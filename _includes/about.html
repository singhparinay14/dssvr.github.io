Overview


The project group DSS-VR (Digitization of Sacred Spaces through Virtual Reality) aims to create an immersive Virtual Reality (VR) application that allows users to explore sacred spaces. With VR, technology has provided the opportunity to not only look at an environment through the screen but to, to a certain degree, actually immerse in and engage with the environment. This can range from entirely fictive spaces to realistic depictions of existing, physical spaces. Many advantages arise from this possibility. With an immense amount of options, creators can create entire worlds and follow their creativity. Spaces also become more accessible. Many people are physically, mentally or financially not able to visit certain spaces that are not accessible or simply too far away. Even physical constraints from the “real world” do not necessarily limit the user in VR. As can be seen, the list of possibilities is exhaustive and by far not complete. This project group is concerned with religious/sacred spaces with a focus on the three Abrahamic religions: Christianity, Islam and Judaism. It is important to note that, at this point, we are only covering Christianity and Islam. With these religion’s vast history and number of followers worldwide, there are various points of interest, i.e., sacred spaces, and a lot of knowledge to consume. A digital, immersive version increases accessibility to these spaces in a way where the user does not only feel like watching something but actually participating. It also allows for a possibly less formal and intimidating environment as well as experiences that exceed reality. While a religion’s follower has an inherent interest in these spaces, people of different beliefs might also have an interest in learning about other religions. They might hear or read something they want to learn more about or even encounter some parts of a religious in their everyday life, e.g., while talking to a co-worker. With the created application, the project group hopes to contribute to research around these topics and answer the question: What are the potential benefits of digitizing sacred spaces through VR and the impact on inter- and intra-faith relationships?
